Expanded Career Highlights

My hands-on experience in construction didn’t just teach me physical labor—it instilled problem-solving instincts, precision, and process awareness that carry over seamlessly into coding. Starting as a laborer, I developed an eye for detail and a sense of how small actions ripple through larger systems. I learned to approach tasks methodically, adapt when things didn’t go as planned, and remain flexible in high-pressure situations. These habits now show up in my frontend workflow—whether I’m debugging layout bugs, organizing components, or fine-tuning user interfaces with SCSS and Bootstrap.

Operating machinery sharpened my spatial awareness and strengthened my ability to plan workflows efficiently—much like optimizing web performance or structuring DOM elements. Anticipating how the movement of materials could affect a day’s outcome built strategic thinking I now rely on when managing assets with tools like Vite or building responsive designs. As I stepped into leadership, communication and team coordination became central. I didn’t just manage a crew—I learned how to sequence tasks, guide different personalities toward a shared vision, and maintain clarity through shifting conditions. These same principles shape how I wireframe user experiences and plan technical milestones, allowing me to blend structure and creativity in both construction and code.
